About Me

Some people are motivated by the "what" and the "how"; I am motivated by the "why".

When I was little, I would take apart pens to inspect the individual pieces. I noticed two things:
1) The ink cartridge or the "clicky button" rotates.
2) The spring is bouncy and fun.

Naturally the springs would bounce away, lost under carpet tendrils. So then I learned two new things:
1) The spring is small but also a vital part of the pen.
2) Everyone else's pen has springs I can borrow to fix my pen.

In the same way, this is how I approach writing and learning code. If I don’t investigate under the hood, I’ll never understand it. If you draw me a diagram, it still won’t make sense until I play with the real code and “lose” things in there. And if I can’t fix my code, I will borrow yours.
